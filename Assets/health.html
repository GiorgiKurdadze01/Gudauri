Health

At a high altitude, the atmospheric pressure decreases and the oxygen saturation of the air decreases. The faster a person climbs, the more likely the air is to cause health problems.

In general, altitude sickness manifests itself from an altitude of 2400 meters above sea level. To compensate for the lack of oxygen, breathing becomes more frequent, the heart beats faster, but the concentration of oxygen in the blood still does not reach the level at sea level. Therefore, there is a risk of developing altitude sickness in the study area, whose height is 2100-2500 meters above sea level. However, if a number of recommendations are taken into account, the risk decreases dramatically.

For acclimatization, i.e. to get used to low atmospheric pressure and lack of oxygen, a person needs a slow, gradual ascent to altitude. Therefore, altitude sickness is mostly manifested by further symptoms.

The first is a headache that does not respond to painkillers. Sometimes it is accompanied by weakness, dizziness, sleep disturbance, loss of appetite, nausea, vomiting, sometimes - visual hallucinations.

In any form of altitude sickness, the first requirement is to stop climbing until the complaints have passed. If symptoms are severe, swelling of the brain or lungs is likely, or vision is blurred, the person should return to the plains as soon as possible.

With a slow, gradual change in altitude, the body adapts to the oxygen-poor environment and the likelihood of developing altitude sickness decreases.

In order to avoid similar problems when climbing to a height in Gudauri, tourists need to be informed about the risks, and in the presence of a problem, it is necessary to seek emergency medical assistance, which cannot be provided directly in the territory of Gudauri.
